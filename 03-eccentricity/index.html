From Circle to Needle

Eccentricity e measures how stretched an ellipse is. Keep a fixed and vary e = c/a.

Tip: drag anywhere on the diagram to swing the ray around focus F1, or use the Ray angle slider.

Try this first

Start with e = 0 (a perfect circle). Slowly slide e toward 1. Predict where the two foci will move before you look — and watch a stay fixed while b shrinks.

What to look for ↓

Real eccentricities for comparison:

  • Earth orbit: e ~ 0.017 (nearly circular)
  • Pluto orbit: e ~ 0.25
  • Halley comet: e ~ 0.97 (very stretched)

Teaching Notes

Core Idea

Eccentricity controls conic shape while preserving the reflection property for ellipses.

Try This

At e=0, compare to a circle. Then move to e=0.9 and describe how the focal points move.

Teacher Prompt

“If e increases, which of a, b, c changes most visibly on-screen?”

Vocabulary

  • Eccentricity: stretch parameter, e=c/a.
  • Circle: special ellipse with e=0.
  • Elongated ellipse: e close to 1.
