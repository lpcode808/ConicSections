Parallel Rays and the Focus

A parabola turns parallel rays into a focused point, and rays emitted from the focus leave parallel. Toggle between the two directions.

Tip: drag the sky to aim the incoming light, or grab the ringed focus point to change the dish's focal length.

Try this first

Watch the parallel rays sweep down and converge at the focus. Now press Mode to reverse the path. Predict where the rays will exit before the toggle happens.

What to look for ↓

Directrix: y = -p. Focus: (0, p). Think satellite dishes (parallel → focus) and flashlights (focus → parallel).

Teaching Notes

Core Idea

Parabolic mirrors convert parallel rays into one focus point, and reverse that path too.

Try This

Switch modes and observe that each path is reversible when reflected over the same surface.

Teacher Prompt

Connect this to flashlights, headlights, and satellite dishes.

Vocabulary

  • Directrix: fixed line in parabola definition.
  • Focus: fixed point paired with directrix.
  • Axis of symmetry: vertical line through focus.
